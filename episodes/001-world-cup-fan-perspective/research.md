# Research Notes

## 2026 World Cup Basics

- 2026 FIFA World Cup will be hosted by Canada, Mexico, and the United States.
- It is the first men's World Cup with 48 teams.
- The format has 12 groups of 4 teams. The top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed teams advance to a 32-team knockout stage.
- FIFA schedule hub: https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/match-schedule-fixtures-results-teams-stadiums
- FIFA format explainer: https://www.fifa.com/en/articles/article-fifa-world-cup-2026-mexico-canada-usa-new-format-tournament-football-soccer

## Fan Perspective Hooks

- The expanded format creates more entry points for casual viewers and national fan bases.
- A North America-hosted World Cup is useful for this show because the hosts/listeners may experience it through time zones, immigrant identity, campus/work conversations, and local event culture.
- The key angle is not prediction. The key angle is participation: who feels allowed to care?

## Messi/Cristiano Ronaldo Hook

- Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo are useful as cultural symbols, not just athletes.
- Messi can anchor themes of genius, fate, tenderness, late-career completion, and Argentina's collective emotion.
- Cristiano Ronaldo can anchor themes of discipline, ambition, body engineering, self-invention, and polarizing greatness.
- Their comparison is a good way to ask: are fans debating football, or debating what kind of excellence they believe in?

## China/Consumer Culture Hooks

- Argentina-related coffee partnerships and football-themed drinks can show how fandom turns into everyday consumption.
- Messi GOAT plushies and World Cup-adjacent merchandise can show how fans buy participation, memory, and identity, not only products.
- Use these as examples rather than making the episode about marketing.
